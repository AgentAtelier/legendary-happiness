Severity: Critical = correctness/nondeterminism; High = architectural debt/real perf cost/missing error handling on a real path/resource risk; Medium = maintainability/duplication/readability/weak tests; Low = nits/style.

## Round summary

The dominant improvement would be a single canonical **TAG-LEGEND** doc + a `compile_scene` lighting-resolution refactor (Q5 + Q4): today the same comments cite any of `Item N`, `B-Series`, `FIX-Series`, `Quality-Series`, `CB-Series` within one file, and the lighting defaults are duplicated across two call-sites that must be kept in lock-step. Fixing both halves the "comment-driven archaeology" readers do when entering this module.

— 20 findings: 2 High (Q1, Q5), 10 Medium, 8 Low. Most quality findings live in `foundry/scene_compiler.py` (the spine emitter), with secondary hotspots in `foundry/room_control.py` (Decision-Point duplication) and the print/logging split that affects all CLI modules.
